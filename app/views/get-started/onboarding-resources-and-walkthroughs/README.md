Beyond getting set up, it’s vital that new starters get to grips with the work being done.

To achieve this, they should speak to the relevant colleagues about the following subjects and areas.

### Programme overview

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-green">Head of UCDA</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-purple">Design Lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-yellow">Research Lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--blue">Feature lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--purple">SME</strong>

It’s important for new starters to discuss the overall programme with a range of colleagues, as each will offer a slightly different but interconnected point of view.

For example, senior leadership figures may focus on high level strategic, operational and delivery goals.  By contrast, an embedded feature team will likely have a more granular understanding of what needs to be developed and the challenges at hand.

#### Key areas to understand

For a new starter to get a full picture, points of discussion should include:

- holistic objectives
- the history of the programme
- the various platforms; CMS, CMS Modern, the replacement service and others
- technology (past, present and future)
- team organisation
- points of contact
- supporting suppliers, teams and individuals
- GDS compliance and our ways of working
- governance procedures
- known issues or challenges
- any other relevant advice

Remember, the work that CPS does and the processes and systems which facilitate it can be complex. It will take time for new starters to solidify and deepen their understanding.

### CPS context

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-green">Head of UCDA</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-purple">Design Lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-yellow">Research Lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--blue">Feature lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--purple">SME</strong>

New starters are encouraged to watch the collection of short videos which focus on the roles and responsibilities of various CPS roles.

We also have our own, more targeted personas to work from when thinking about user needs and approaching design challenges. Speak to the User research lead to get access to these.

#### Key areas of understanding

There is no substitute for direct user engagement, whether via user research or by shadowing CPS colleagues in person for a day. All new starters are encouraged to speak to their teams about how they can best understand our users.

Understanding the core roles, responsibilities, needs and challenges of our users is vital to achieving good design.

### Quarterly planning

Quarterly planning is essential for the delivery of our work, setting our overall direction and the interaction between various teams every three months.

The new starter will need to be invited to quarterly planning meetings, and will also contribute to the quarterly planning Miro board.

### CPS Design Manual

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-purple">Design Lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-green">Head of UCDA</strong>

The CPS Design Manual (password: eagle) offers every member of the design team a repository of:

- preferred styles, components and patterns
- style and pattern guidance for content design
- design guidance more broadly
- best practice and decisions taken
- design histories

#### Key areas of understanding

As a rule, we always seek to adhere to the overall GDS guidance and standards.

However, the CPS Design Manual allows us to tailor this to our own design work, so we have a dedicated version that reflects the needs of CPS and the journeys we’ve been on. The Friday Design Guild meetings often relate to the Design Manual and what needs to be figured out to codify design decisions and promote consistency.
