Who new starters need to meet during the onboarding process.

Knowing who you’re working with is an important early step. Read through the roles on this page to understand the key people involved, and how they’ll be involved in welcoming a new starter.

### Onboarding partner

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--grey">Onboarding partner</strong>

Every new starter joining the CPS design team should be assigned a dedicated Onboarding partner to give one-to-one support during the welcome process.

#### Their role

The partner is typically assigned to a new starter by their supplier. It’s important that they are close enough to the work being done so they can explain it clearly.

Along with several other key individuals they will bring new starters into the team and prepare them for work.

They act as the go-to person for friendly advice while the new starter is settling in, helping them to:

- get set up
- gain an early understanding of the problem space
- understand user needs and operational requirements
- understand any current design thinking
- meet other key team members

### Head of User-Centred Design and Analysis

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--grey">Head of UCDA</strong>

#### Their role

The Head of UCDA is a key individual for all new starters to meet and build a collaborative relationship with. They lead the overall strategy for design, user research, business analysis and digital accessibility professions.

For new starters, their role is to explain key aspects, including:

- giving perspective on higher level design strategy
- giving insight around the context of the work and how it fits into the wider programme

### Design Lead

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--grey">Design Lead</strong>

#### Their role

The Design Lead is responsible for the overall direction and delivery of design.

New starters will work closely with them to:

- understand how their individual and team work fits into the larger project
- identify areas of crossover between different teams, and consider ways to collaborate
- consider and document best practice within CPS UCD

### User Research Lead

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--grey">Research Lead</strong>

#### Their role

The User Research Lead can help new starters to understand how user research and its insights inform the programme and design iteration.

New starters will work with them to:

- understand what user research already exists
- understand the key user needs and insights we’re drawing from
- ensure their own questions for users are incorporated into future rounds of research

### Feature lead

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--grey">Feature lead</strong>

#### Their role

Each major part of the service we are updating is led by a feature lead.

This person has an in-depth understanding of their particular service area or product, how it links up with the rest of the service and the challenges and opportunities in play. They are also closely aligned with the cycle of development updates, which may be useful information for design team members to understand.

### Subject matter expert (SME)

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--grey">SME</strong>

#### Their role

There will be situations where a subject matter expert (SME) is best placed to support a new starter. This could be a Delivery lead, Product owner, Technical architect, or other stakeholder with specialist knowledge, skills or permissions relating to a specific area of the overall service, or a good general understanding of how its processes work.

They can also be useful sources of information about how our users are currently experiencing the service, and where pain points are.

The ‘SME’ tag applies to any colleague who is needed for onboarding, but isn’t covered by the other roles listed on this page.

#### The first meeting

Priority level for meeting: Medium to high, depending on project and need
