
We use a wide range of tools and platforms to realise our design and research work.

The new starter will need access to all of the following.

### Microsoft Outlook and Teams access

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--blue">Feature lead</strong>

#### What we use it for

Microsoft Teams is our main communication channel, and the platform we use for all our meetings. It should have a synchronised calendar with Microsoft Outlook, which is used for emails.

#### Getting access

The new starter should automatically be assigned their Outlook and Teams accounts when they get their CPS email address.

The Onboarding partner should check with them to make sure they can access both platforms.

### Meeting invitations

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-green">Head of UCDA</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-purple">Design Lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-yellow">Research Lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--blue">Future team lead</strong>

#### What we use them for

CPS teams will usually have their own regular meetings and ceremony cadences, which repeat every week, month and quarter. Various design-focused meetings are also vital to the delivery of our work.

#### Getting access

It’s up to the Onboarding partner and the new starter themself to work with the respective leads to ensure nothing is missed, and to keep an eye out for any other meetings the new starter should be part of.

Some of the key regular meetings new starters will probably need to join include:

- feature team standups
- other recurring Feature team meetings or ceremonies
- Wednesday Design Feedback session
- Friday Design Guild session
- UCD sessions for collaboration and discussion
- quarterly planning sessions
- other recurring meetings hosted by their specific supplier

It is the new starter’s responsibility to figure out which meetings are vital to attend, and which can be treated with more flexibility. If they are unsure or are concerned about having too many meetings they should discuss with their team leads.

### Miro

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-purple">Design Lead</strong>

#### What we use it for

Miro boards are frequently used for planning, mapping, research and design work.

#### Getting access

The new starter should be assigned Miro by their onboarding team.

However, be aware that licences are sometimes limited. Typically, the Design lead will need to request Miro access from the Associate delivery manager at CPS.

### GitHub and Heroku

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-green">Head of UCDA</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-purple">Design Lead</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--purple">SME</strong>

#### What we use it for

GitHub is used for build prototypes, while Heroku is used for prototype hosting.

#### Getting Access

New starters can download and install GitHub onto their machine.

They can request access to both GitHub and Heroku from the Design Lead, who will also help them to get set up and align with existing CPS prototypes.

### Figma

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-purple">Design Lead</strong>

#### What we use it for

Figma is a platform used for designing, testing, collaboration and presentation.

Designers sometimes use Figma to create representations of screens and workflows, which other designers contribute to.

#### Getting access

New starters will need to be invited to CPS Figma by the Design lead.

### Sharepoint

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--blue">Feature lead</strong>

#### What we use it for

We use Sharepoint to store and share documents and other work resources.

#### Getting access

New starters should automatically be assigned Sharepoint access as part of their Microsoft Teams and Outlook setup.

The Feature lead for each area the new starter is working in will need to invite them to the relevant Sharepoint area.

### JIRA and Confluence

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--blue">Feature lead</strong>

#### What we use it for

We use JIRA to manage work delivery, with tickets being made to represent the things team members are working on. JIRA is central to sprint planning and quarterly planning.

Confluence is used to record key information, including dev release updates and design histories.

#### Getting access

The Feature lead for each area the new starter is working in will need to invite them to the relevant JIRA board and Confluence pages.

### Development environments

<strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--light-blue">Onboarding partner</strong> <strong class="govuk-tag govuk-tag--blue">Feature lead</strong>

#### What we use it for

There are three kinds of development environments: Development (Dev), Test and Production (Prod).

Development contains the code the developers are working on. This is released to Test, where initial testing takes place. When ready for user research it is released in Production.

#### Getting access

New starters should speak to their supplier technical lead to get access to the development environments.
